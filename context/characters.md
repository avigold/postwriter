# Main Characters

## Protagonist — working name: Callum Harker

Early thirties. Grew up in a council estate in Sunderland. Got into LSE on a scholarship, then into a mid-tier hedge fund, then — through sheer competence and a talent for pattern recognition — into the orbit of people whose family money predates the industrial revolution.

He's not naive. He knows what these people are. But he's pragmatic and observant, and there's a part of him that finds the excess anthropologically fascinating. He code-switches well enough to pass at dinner parties but never loses the awareness that he's performing. Dry, understated wit. Not a hero by nature — he'd prefer to keep his head down and do his job. The novel forces him not to.

His analytical mind is what leads him to the black meat discovery: he notices a pattern in the financial flows behind the supply chain before he ever looks at the meat itself.

## Love interest — working name: Suki Cheung

A food journalist and critic. Hong Kong-born, London-based. Sharp, ambitious, genuinely talented. She's writing a feature on the black meat trend when she and Callum cross paths. She has access to the culinary world that he doesn't, and he has access to the financial world that she doesn't.

Their relationship should be genuinely romantic and sexual — not just functional to the plot. She is not a damsel. She has her own arc and her own courage. The attraction is intellectual as much as physical: they are both people who see clearly in a world that doesn't want to be seen.

## Antagonist(s)

Not a single villain. The antagonist is a class — the ultra-wealthy who eat black meat and transform. But there should be specific faces:

- A tech billionaire (think a composite of the worst tendencies of Silicon Valley) who becomes an evangelist for the meat and its "cognitive enhancement" properties. He's charming, articulate, and increasingly terrifying.
- A social media influencer who documents his own transformation with a kind of ecstatic narcissism, unaware of what he's becoming.
- The supply chain: whoever is providing the meat. This should remain partly obscured — the horror is more effective if the source is suggested rather than fully explained.

## Supporting

- Callum's colleague at the fund — someone who eats the meat and changes, providing Callum with a close-up view of the transformation.
- A chef who knows what the meat is and is paid enough not to care, until he does care.
