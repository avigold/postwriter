# Thematic Guidelines

These themes should arise from the story. They should never be stated by the narrator.

## Consumption

The central metaphor: the ultra-wealthy literally consuming people. This is already what they do — economically, politically, culturally — and the black meat makes it physical. The novel should let this metaphor do its work without underlining it.

## Class as biology

The meat creates a literal biological divide between rich and poor. This mirrors real dynamics where wealth buys health, longevity, cognitive enhancement. The satire is in the acceleration, not the direction.

## Complicity and observation

Callum's position — adjacent to wealth, benefiting from proximity to it, uncomfortable but not leaving — mirrors the reader's own relationship to the systems being satirised. He's not innocent. His hands aren't clean. The novel shouldn't let him (or the reader) off the hook.

## The body

Food, sex, violence — the novel is obsessed with the body. What goes into it, what it does, what it's capable of, what it becomes. The transformation should be felt physically in the prose.

## Romance as the human counterweight

Callum and Suki's relationship is the thing that stays human while everything else deteriorates. Their connection — intellectual, sexual, emotional — is the novel's anchor. It should feel real and specific enough that the reader has something to lose when the world goes to hell.
